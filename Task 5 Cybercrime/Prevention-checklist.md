*Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)*

---

1.	Use strong and unique passwords
Create passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols, and avoid using the same password for multiple accounts.
2.	Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Turn on 2FA for important accounts such as email, social media, and banking apps for an extra layer of security.
3.	Do not click suspicious links
Avoid opening unknown links received through SMS, email, or social media messages claiming prizes, urgent account issues, or offers.
4.	Verify unknown contacts online
If someone asks for personal information or money through social media or messaging apps, verify their identity before responding.
5.	Avoid sharing personal information publicly
Do not post sensitive details like phone numbers, address, student ID, or travel plans openly on social media.
6.	UPI Safety Tip – Never share your UPI PIN
Your UPI PIN is confidential. No bank or payment app like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm will ever ask for it.
7.	UPI Safety Tip – Remember: entering your PIN means paying money
If you receive a “Collect Request” or QR code request, entering your PIN will send money, not receive it. Always check the request carefully.
8.	Use secure Wi-Fi networks
Avoid logging into banking or payment apps on public Wi-Fi networks in cafes, malls, or hostels.
9.	Keep your apps and phone updated
Install updates regularly to fix security vulnerabilities and protect your device from malware.
10.	Report cyber fraud immediately
If you face any cybercrime or online fraud, report it on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Cybercrime Helpline: 1930 as soon as possible.
